# Oil Price Volatility & GCC Fiscal Impact Analysis 🛢️
**Tools:** Python (Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn) | Power BI

## Project Overview
Analyzed Brent crude oil price trends (2018–2026) and their 
downstream impact on GCC government fiscal budgets across 
Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain, and Oman.

## Key Findings
- Lowest price: $9.12/barrel (April 2020 — COVID crash)
- Highest price: $138.21/barrel (April 2026)
- Qatar most fiscally resilient — surplus in 7 out of 8 years
- Bahrain most vulnerable — deficit every single year
- 2022 Russia-Ukraine war caused the biggest price spike

## Visualizations
1. Brent crude price trend with OPEC+ event markers
2. Annual average price and volatility by year
3. GCC fiscal breakeven vs actual price comparison
4. Surplus/deficit heatmap by country and year

## Data Sources
- Brent crude prices: FRED (Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis)
- GCC fiscal breakeven: IMF Regional Economic Outlook
- OPEC+ decisions: OPEC press release archive
